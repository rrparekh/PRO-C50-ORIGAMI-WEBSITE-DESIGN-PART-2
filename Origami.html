Interesting Facts about Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  • The species' post-hatching life span is just 4 to 5 months.

Interesting Facts about Cicadas

  • Most Cicadas have red-orange eyes
  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.
  • They don't bite or sting; they do however pierce and suck.

Interesting Facts about Pigeons

  • Pigeons are considered to be one of the most intelligent birds on the planet.
  • They are able to recognize themselves in the mirror and they recognize all letters of the English alphabet.
  • Pigeons are herbivores (plant-eaters). Their diet consists of seeds, fruit and various plants.

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  • An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
//

Interesting Facts about Bears

  • Bears can run twice as fast as humans can.
  • They are shy, their normal response to any perceived danger is to run away.
  • Bears have great memories, handy for remembering the locations of food sources.
